Variant 1 — Pill + dot halo (outside only)

The pill itself is clean white. Around it, a single layer of dots emanates outward and fades. Dots are uniform size, density decreases toward the edge via a soft mask. Feels like the pill is sitting on the existing dot-grid, but slightly emphasized.

Variant 2 — Dots printed inside the pill, sparse at edges

The dot pattern lives inside the pill, dense at the center and thinning toward the pill's curved edges. Reads as a textured “printed” button. No external halo.

Variant 3 — Two-layer halo (bitmap fade out)

Closer to the “bitmap that gets smaller toward the edges” idea. Two halo layers: medium dots in a tight ring just outside the pill, then smaller dots in a wider, sparser outer ring. Reads like the pill is shedding pixels as it dissolves into the background.

Variant 4 (A) — Gray pill, noise-roughened edge

Solid gray pill where the rim has been chewed up by an SVG turbulence filter. Edges feel like they crumble into specks. CSS-only, approximate halftone — the "vibe" version. Whole pill (including text) sits inside the filter.

Variant 5 (B) — Gray pill, true halftone dot edge

Real round dots breaking off the rim. Built with an SVG filter that dilates the pill outward, thresholds a fine noise pattern into round dots, masks them to the rim band, and tints them with the pill color. Closest to the reference images you shared.
